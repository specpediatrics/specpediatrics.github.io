Respiratory Core

The Respiratory Core builds the physiologic and clinical foundation that every trainee in Pediatric Critical Care and Pulmonology must command. From first-principles ventilator mechanics to advanced waveform interpretation, from the acutely wheezing toddler to the child arriving home on a tracheostomy and ventilator — these eight sessions move from foundational respiratory physiology to life-threatening respiratory failure.

PICU Core

The PICU Core spans the full intellectual breadth of pediatric critical illness. From the hemodynamic physiology of septic shock and the neuroscience of refractory status epilepticus, to the pharmacology of sedation, the biochemistry of acute organ failure, and the systems thinking required for safe, high-quality intensive care — these 14 sessions, organized across five disciplines, form the essential cognitive foundation of the pediatric intensivist.

Complex Care Core
