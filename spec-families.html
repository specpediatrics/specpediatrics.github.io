You Have a Team Behind You

These resources exist because of your questions. You are not expected to know all of this before you leave — your team will make sure you are ready.

📞

Your Care Coordinator

Save your SPEC care coordinator's number before leaving the hospital. No question is too small — they are there specifically for you. Call before you worry alone at 2am.

📱

Telehealth for Rural Families

If you live more than 90 minutes from Lubbock, most follow-up visits can be done by video. Ask your team to set up telehealth before discharge so it's ready when you get home.

🏥

Your Nearest ER as a Partner

Write your child's tube type, size, and settings on a wallet card. Your nearest ER can help with G-tube replacements and trach emergencies — call ahead when driving in.

⚡

Register With Your Utility

Call your electric company and register as life-support dependent. This is free, speeds up power restoration during outages, and should be done before your child comes home.

🚒

Notify Your Local EMS

Let your local fire and EMS know your child's address and that they use a trach or vent. Many rural West Texas EMS teams welcome this so they can prepare before any call.

👨‍👩‍👧

Find Your Community

Connecting with other trach, vent, and G-tube families changes everything. Ask your social worker about parent communities — online groups and local connections both help.

🌐

Spanish Language Support / Apoyo en Español

All SPEC family materials are being made available in Spanish. Ask your care team for printed materials in Spanish, or request a certified medical interpreter for all teaching sessions. You have the right to understand your child's care fully.

Todos los materiales de SPEC para familias están disponibles en español. Tiene el derecho a entender completamente el cuidado de su hijo. Solicite un intérprete médico certificado para todas sus sesiones de enseñanza.
