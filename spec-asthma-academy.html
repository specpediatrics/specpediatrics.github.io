Welcome to SPEC Asthma Academy

Start Here — Who This Course Is For

Asthma affects an estimated 300 million people worldwide and remains a leading cause of preventable pediatric hospitalization. Despite evidence-based guidelines, diagnosis is frequently delayed, inhaler technique is routinely unchecked, and SABA-only treatment — now recognized as inadequate — remains widespread. This course is built for everyone who cares for children with asthma and wants to do it better.

Inhaler & Device Teaching — Video Library

Watch, Practice, Demonstrate

Trusted device-demonstration videos from Children's Hospital Colorado reinforce hands-on inhaler teaching. These videos are intended to supplement — not replace — live demonstration and return demonstration with a clinician.

📌 How to use this section: Watch the video → Practice with the actual device → Demonstrate technique back to a clinician → Recheck at every follow-up visit. A watched video does not replace hands-on coaching.
💨 MDI with Spacer (Valved Holding Chamber) Core
Demonstrates the pressurized metered-dose inhaler used with a spacer device — the preferred delivery method for ICS in children using a pMDI. Covers priming, shaking, timing, and breath-hold.
Audience: Resident + Family

👶 MDI with Spacer and Mask Infants/Toddlers
Demonstrates spacer use with a fitted face mask for infants and young children who cannot form a seal around a mouthpiece. Correct mask seal and breathing pattern are critical for adequate drug delivery.
Audience: Resident + Family
▶ Watch Video 📄 Written Guide
🏥 Source: Children's Hospital Colorado
🔵 RediHaler (Breath-Actuated MDI) Device-Specific
The RediHaler is a breath-actuated device that releases medication automatically upon inhalation, eliminating the coordination challenge of a standard pMDI. Spacer is not used. Technique differs significantly from a standard MDI.
Audience: Resident + Family
▶ Watch Video 📄 Written Guide
🏥 Source: Children's Hospital Colorado
🌬️ Respimat (Soft Mist Inhaler) Device-Specific
The Respimat produces a slow-moving aerosol cloud (soft mist), requiring less inspiratory effort than DPIs and allowing more time for inhalation than standard MDIs. Used for tiotropium in pediatric asthma and for some combination inhalers.
Audience: Resident + Family
▶ Watch Video 📄 Written Guide
🏥 Source: Children's Hospital Colorado
🌀 Diskus / Flexhaler / Ellipta / Twisthaler DPI Devices
Dry-powder inhalers (DPIs) require a strong, fast inhalation to disperse medication. No spacer is used. Loading mechanism and mouthpiece design vary by device. Generally suitable for children ≥5–6 years with adequate inspiratory flow.
Audience: Resident + Family
▶ Watch Videos 📄 Written Guides
🏥 Source: Children's Hospital Colorado
💧 Nebulizer Acute/Home Use
Demonstrates proper nebulizer setup, medication preparation, mask vs. mouthpiece use, and treatment duration. Note: GINA 2025 recommends reserving nebulizers for situations where spacer use is not feasible or effective.
Audience: Resident + Family

🏥 Source: Children's Hospital Colorado
For Residents
Teaching with Videos
  • Use the video before or after live technique teaching as preparation or reinforcement
  • Pause at key steps and ask the learner or family to identify what was just demonstrated
  • Ask: "What common mistakes do you think people make with this device?"
  • Perform teach-back immediately after — ask the patient or family to demonstrate with the actual device
  • Document: "Inhaler technique reviewed with demonstration and return demonstration; supplemental device video from Children's Hospital Colorado provided."
For Families
Watch at Home, Then Practice
  • Watch the video for your child's specific device at home — not just once, but before each refill season
  • Practice with the inhaler in your hands while watching
  • Bring the inhaler and spacer to every follow-up visit so your clinician can check your technique
  • Technique drifts over time — even experienced users benefit from a recheck every 6–12 months
  • If the video and your inhaler look different, call your pharmacy or provider — confirm you have the right device

Digital Tools & Resources

Useful Asthma Apps

Selected digital tools for asthma management, education, and monitoring — evaluated for clinical credibility. These tools supplement but do not replace guideline-based care or clinical judgment.

🌐
GINA Asthma App
iOS · Android · Free
The official Global Initiative for Asthma mobile app provides direct access to GINA strategy content, treatment step algorithms, and asthma action plan templates — updated annually. An essential companion for any clinician managing asthma by guideline.
GINA OfficialTreatment StepsClinician
Available at ginasthma.org. Content mirrors the current GINA Report — verify version matches the most recent update.
📱
Propeller Health
iOS · Android · Sensor-based
A sensor-connected platform that attaches to inhalers (SABA and controller) and tracks medication use in real time. Provides adherence data, trigger mapping, and personalized feedback. Research has shown improvement in controller adherence and reduction in SABA use with this platform.
AdherenceSensor-ConnectedPatient/Clinician
Requires compatible sensor hardware. Most relevant for patients with poor adherence or frequent SABA use — a key risk marker per GINA 2025.
📋
Asthma Tracker (Asthma MD)
iOS · Android
A symptom and peak flow diary with trigger logging, medication reminders, and physician-shareable reports. Useful for families monitoring day-to-day control and documenting patterns between clinical visits.
Symptom DiaryPeak FlowFamily
Not a diagnostic tool. Best used in conjunction with a written asthma action plan and regular clinical review.
🏠
AirVisual (IQAir)
iOS · Android · Free
Real-time outdoor air quality monitoring using the Air Quality Index (AQI) and particulate matter data. Particularly relevant for families in West Texas and Eastern New Mexico where dust storms, wildfire smoke, and seasonal pollen events are common asthma triggers.
Air QualityTrigger AvoidanceFamily
Complements trigger education — teach families to check AQI before outdoor activities, especially in high-pollution or fire days.
📊
Zyrtec AllergyCast
iOS · Android · Free
Daily pollen count and allergen forecast by location. Useful for allergic asthma — helping families anticipate high-pollen days when allergen-triggered exacerbations are more likely. Covers tree, grass, and weed pollen as well as mold spore levels.
Pollen TrackingAllergic TriggersFamily
Pollen avoidance strategies are most relevant in sensitized patients — confirm sensitization before advising pollen-specific avoidance per GINA 2025 guidance.
🩺
Epocrates / UpToDate
iOS · Android · Subscription
Clinical decision support tools widely used by residents and physicians. Epocrates provides quick drug dosing and interaction reference; UpToDate provides comprehensive, evidence-graded clinical summaries on asthma diagnosis and management updated regularly.
Clinical ReferenceDrug DosingClinician
UpToDate content is periodically updated and may differ from the most recent GINA annual revision — cross-reference with official GINA resources for guideline-aligned recommendations.

For Faculty & Preceptors

Teaching This Curriculum

Practical guidance for using SPEC Asthma Academy in clinic-based teaching, noon conference, and inpatient rounds — including observation checklists and direct assessment tools.

Clinic Teaching
How to Use This in Ambulatory Settings
  • Assign one module per week of ambulatory rotation — learners review independently, debrief at pre-clinic
  • Use the Quick Reference checklists as structured observation tools during a patient encounter
  • Ask residents to perform a live inhaler technique check with every asthma patient — observe and score
  • Use Module 3 (preschool diagnosis) for any child under 6 presenting with recurrent wheeze — discuss uncertainty openly
  • After any ED visit or hospitalization, review the discharge checklist together before the patient leaves
Direct Observation & Assessment
Mini-CEX & OSCE Ideas
  • Inhaler Teaching OSCE: observe resident teaching a standardized patient to use a pMDI + spacer — score technique, teach-back, and documentation
  • Discharge Counseling OSCE: resident counsels a family after an ED visit — action plan, return precautions, follow-up
  • Spirometry Interpretation Station: provide a pre/post-bronchodilator spirometry — ask resident to interpret and apply GINA criteria
  • Preschool Diagnosis Station: present a 3-year-old with recurrent wheeze — resident must develop and explain a diagnostic approach
  • Difficult Asthma Station: present an "uncontrolled" child — resident must identify modifiable factors before escalating treatment
Noon Conference & Inpatient Rounds
Short Teaching Formats
  • 5-Minute Pearl: "Why SABA-only treatment is no longer acceptable" — use Module 1 opening argument
  • 10-Minute Device Demo: bring three devices (MDI, spacer, DPI) — live demonstration with audience participation
  • Morning Report Case: use one of the 6 Module 10 case vignettes — structured discussion with learning points
  • Grand Rounds Topic: "GINA 2025 vs. NAEPP 2020 — What Changed and What Matters for Your Patients"
Assessing Resident Performance
What Good Looks Like
  • A well-prepared resident diagnoses asthma using objective criteria, not just a label from a prior chart
  • Demonstrates inhaler technique accurately themselves before teaching a family
  • Understands that "poor control" requires assessment of technique and adherence before escalation
  • Provides a written asthma action plan at every eligible encounter — not just at discharge
  • Recognizes SABA overuse as a warning sign requiring same-day reassessment of controller therapy
