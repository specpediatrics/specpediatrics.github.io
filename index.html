Our Mission

Education that bridges the gap between trainee and patient.

The Southwest Pediatric Education Collaborative (SPEC) was created to support trainee-led pediatric education, collaborative learning, and scholarly activity.

Through mentorship, innovation, and shared educational work, SPEC helps transform ideas into teaching tools, community resources, simulation projects, and academic scholarship.

"We believe the best pediatric education is built by the people closest to the patients — the trainees themselves."

Trainee-Driven

Residents and fellows lead every project — with mentorship and structure to turn ideas into real educational impact.

Community-Centered

Serving families across West Texas and Eastern New Mexico, including Spanish-speaking and rural communities far from academic centers.

Scholarship-Ready

Projects are designed to grow into presentations, publications, and curriculum — building your academic portfolio while helping patients.

What We Do

Three Pillars of Collaborative Work

Every SPEC project falls under one of three areas — each designed to create lasting educational value.

🎓
01

Peer Teaching

Residents and trainees create practical teaching tools, quick references, case discussions, and learning materials for fellow learners — from bedside guides to simulation curricula.

👨‍👩‍👧
02

Family & Community Education

SPEC supports projects that improve communication, health literacy, and educational resources for patients, families, and caregivers — in the languages and formats they need.

📖
03

Scholarship & Innovation

Educational ideas developed through SPEC can grow into scholarly projects, conference presentations, curriculum design, and publications — building your CV while building the field.
